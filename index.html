Life insurance is the foundation of any solid financial plan. It ensures your family is taken care of financially if something unexpected happens to you.

  • Term Life Insurance — Affordable coverage for a set period (10, 20, or 30 years). Ideal for young families or those with a mortgage.
  • Whole Life Insurance — Permanent coverage that never expires, with a cash value component that grows over time.
  • Universal Life Insurance — Flexible premiums and a tax-advantaged investment component — protection + savings in one.
  • No Medical / Simplified Issue — Coverage without a medical exam, perfect for those with health conditions.

A serious diagnosis shouldn't mean a financial crisis. These coverages protect your income and lifestyle when health challenges arise.

  • Critical Illness Insurance — Receive a lump-sum, tax-free payment if you're diagnosed with a covered condition (cancer, heart attack, stroke, and more). Use it however you need.
  • Disability Insurance — Replaces a portion of your income if you become unable to work due to illness or injury. Available for both short-term and long-term needs.

Did you know? 1 in 3 Canadians will experience a disability lasting 90+ days before age 65.

Gaps in provincial health coverage can cost you thousands. Supplemental health plans cover what government plans don't.

  • Extended Health Care — Prescription drugs, vision, paramedical services (physiotherapy, massage, chiropractic), and more.
  • Dental Insurance — Routine cleanings, fillings, crowns, and orthodontics depending on your plan level.
  • AD&D (Accidental Death & Dismemberment) — Pays a benefit if you die or suffer a severe injury in an accident.

Whether you're going on vacation or bringing family to Canada, travel insurance gives you peace of mind wherever you go.

  • Emergency Medical Travel Insurance — Covers hospital stays, doctor visits, and evacuation costs while abroad.
  • Trip Cancellation & Interruption — Get reimbursed if your trip is cancelled or cut short due to covered reasons.
  • Super Visa Insurance — Required for parents and grandparents visiting Canada on a Super Visa. Must provide at least $100,000 in coverage for a minimum of 1 year.
  • Visitor to Canada Insurance — Emergency medical coverage for family or friends visiting from abroad.

Smart saving strategies can dramatically grow your wealth over time. Let's make sure your money is working as hard as you do.

  • RRSP (Registered Retirement Savings Plan) — Reduce your taxable income today while saving for retirement. Contributions are tax-deductible and grow tax-deferred. Annual limit: 18% of earned income (up to $32,490 for 2026).
  • RESP (Registered Education Savings Plan) — Save for your child's post-secondary education and receive up to $7,200 in government grants (CESG). Lifetime limit of $50,000 per beneficiary.
  • TFSA (Tax-Free Savings Account) — Save and invest tax-free. Withdrawals are tax-free too — great for any goal: home, retirement, or emergency fund. 2026 annual limit: $7,500; cumulative lifetime limit: $102,000.
  • FHSA (First Home Savings Account) — A powerful new account for first-time home buyers. Contributions are tax-deductible (like an RRSP) and withdrawals for a qualifying home purchase are tax-free (like a TFSA). Annual limit: $8,000; lifetime limit: $40,000.
